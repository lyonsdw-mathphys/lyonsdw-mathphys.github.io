Reading Problems will be graded for preparation and completeness, not for correctness. In order to receive credit, all of your solutions, correct or incorrect, must show evidence of your reasoning. Even a true/false or fill-in-the-blank question requires at least a comment that demonstrates your understanding. Complete sentences are not required for reading problem solutions, but a final answer with no work shown receives zero credit. If you cannot complete a solution, you may receive credit by writing one or more complete sentences that explain precisely where you are stuck.
