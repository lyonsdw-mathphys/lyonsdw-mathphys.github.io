Reading Problems

The purpose of Reading Problems (see the section "Reading and Problem List") is to help you understand the material in the reading. You are not expected to solve all of the Reading Problems perfectly, but you are expected to try.

Reading Problems will be graded for preparation and completeness, not for correctness. In order to receive credit, all of your solutions, correct or incorrect, must show evidence of your reasoning. Even a true/false or fill-in-the-blank question requires at least a comment that demonstrates your understanding. Complete sentences are not required for reading problem solutions, but a final answer with no work shown receives zero credit.

There are two ways for a Reading Problem solution to earn full credit:

  • Whether or not it is correct, your solution shows evidence of your thinking and understanding.
  • In place of a solution, you may receive credit by writing one or more complete sentences that explain precisely where you are stuck.

Submit Reading Problems online on Canvas. Your work may be typed or written by hand. Your work must be submitted as a single pdf file, whether it is handwritten or typed (pdf scanning is a built-in utility for iphones and android phones). Work is not accepted late. There are no makeup assignments.

Reading Problems Grade Rubric
Category Description
Complete All of the Reading Problems are attempted. Each solution shows evidence of your thoughts and understanding, or gives a statement, using one or more complete sentences, that articulates precisely where you are stuck.
Partially complete One or more solutions are not complete or some of the submission is not clear enough to be readable.
No credit Solutions show no effort to express the main ideas, are unreadable, or are submitted after the deadline.
