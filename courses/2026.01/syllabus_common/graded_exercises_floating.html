Graded Exercises

Reading the text(s) and solving problems is the largest part of your work in this course, and most of that work takes place outside of class. You will submit written work to demonstrate evidence of your progress in reading and problem solving and to prepare for each class meeting. This work will be graded for effort and completeness, but not for correctness (see the rubric below).

Most of the sections (or chapters) of the text(s) (see the Reading and Exercises section above) are spread out over more than one class meeting. Class days are labeled A, B, C, etc to indicate where you should aim to be within each section or chapter. 

  A  the first half of the reading and exercises
  B  the second half of the reading and exercises
  C  more solutions not yet submitted for A and B
  D  more solutions not yet submitted for A, B, C

The length expectation for each submission is one page minimum, but you are allowed to submit more than one page. There is no specified list of problems for submitted written work for any given section or chapter; it is your job to choose about a page of solutions for each assignment. Keep in mind that you are responsible for understanding and proficiency in all of the reading and exercises, even though you will submit only a fraction of the solutions.

Your work may be typed or written by hand. Your work must be submitted as a single pdf file, whether it is handwritten or typed. Work is not accepted late. There are no makeup assignments.

Solutions will be graded for preparation and completeness, not for correctness. For full credit, solutions must show evidence that you have read the material and must show evidence of your thinking and understanding. You are not expected to solve all of the problems perfectly, but you are expected to try.

Exercise Solutions Grading Rubric
Category Description
Complete At least one page of written work is submitted. Solutions show evidence that you have read the material and made a sincere attempt to solve the problems. Solutions show evidence of your thinking and understanding.
Partially complete Any one or more of the following apply: less than one page is submitted; one or more solutions are not complete; one or more solutions disregard the reading; one or more solutions do not show evidence of your thinking; some of the submission is not readable.
No credit Solutions show no effort to express the main ideas, are unreadable, or are submitted after the deadline.
