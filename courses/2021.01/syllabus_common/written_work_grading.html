Writing Assignments

Writing assignments will be collected and graded approximately once each week. Writing assignments must be typed and must use appropriate mathematical symbols and typesetting conventions. Handwritten solutions will not be accepted. Your writing assignment will be graded for correctness in the following ways: for correct use of vocabulary and notation; for correct logical flow in your argument; and for the correct final conclusion(s).

Writing Assignment Scoring Rubric
Category Description
Complete and correct The writing assignment is typed, is complete and correct, shows appropriate work, and uses correct vocabulary and notation.
Substantive progress The work shows understanding of the appropriate facts, methods, and vocabulary, but does not has a small flaw (for example, incorrect use of vocabulary, notation, or an error in calculation). The writing assignment shows understanding of method(s) and issues involved.
3 points The writing assignment has a "medium" flaw (too large to be "small"), or several small flaws.
2 points Response shows a start on the right direction, or has some part of the problem right, but largely goes wrong---too much wrong to earn 3 points.
1 point There is some attempt at the problem; there is plausible evidence that some aspect of the problem is grasped.
0 points No writing assignment is submitted, a writing assignment is submitted but is not typed, or claims are made with no supporting justification, or the writing assignment shows no plausible evidence that some key aspect of the problem is grasped.
