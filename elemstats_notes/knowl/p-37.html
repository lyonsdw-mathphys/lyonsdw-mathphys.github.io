A special case SD formula: Suppose that all of the entries of a list \(X=A,A,A,\ldots,A,B,B,B,\ldots B\) are one of two values \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) with \(A\lt B\text{,}\) and suppose there are \(r\) entries with value \(A\) and \(s\) entries with value \(B\text{.}\) The SD of the list \(X\) is given by the following formula.

\begin{equation} \text{SD}(X)= (B-A)\sqrt{\frac{r}{r+s}\frac{s}{r+s}} = (B-A)\frac{\sqrt{rs}}({r+s}\tag{4.6} \end{equation}
